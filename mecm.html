Motion in a straight line, also known as rectilinear motion, describes the movement of an object along a single dimension. This could be along a straight road, a railway track, or even vertically upwards.

Uniform Motion: Occurs when an object moves with constant velocity. This means it covers equal distances in equal intervals of time.

Uniformly Accelerated Motion: Occurs when an object's velocity changes by the same amount in equal intervals of time. This is often observed when a constant force acts on an object.
