In physics, vectors are quantities that possess both magnitude (size) and direction. Unlike scalars, which are simply numbers, vectors require both a numerical value and a specified direction for complete description.

Vectors are often represented graphically as arrows, where the length of the arrow represents the magnitude and the arrowhead indicates the direction.

Vectors can be broken down into components along perpendicular axes, making it easier to analyze and manipulate them.
