Topic Description

Chemical bonding describes the forces that hold atoms together to form molecules and compounds. These forces arise from the interactions between electrons in the outermost energy levels of atoms.

Ionic Bonds: Formed by the electrostatic attraction between oppositely charged ions (cations and anions)

Covalent Bonds: Formed by the sharing of electrons between atoms.

Metallic Bonds: Found in metals, where a "sea" of delocalized electrons is shared among a lattice of positively charged metal ions.
