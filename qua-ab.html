Topic Description

A quadratic equation is a polynomial equation of the second degree, meaning it involves a variable raised to the power of two.

The standard form of a quadratic equation is: ax² + bx + c = 0 where: a, b, and c are constants (coefficients) a cannot be equal to 0 (otherwise, it wouldn't be a quadratic equation) x is the unknown variable

The values of 'x' that satisfy the quadratic equation

The part of the quadratic formula under the square root (b² - 4ac). It determines the nature of the roots (real, imaginary, or equal)
