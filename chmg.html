Topic Description

Gases exhibit unique characteristics due to the significant space between their constituent particles. Key properties include:

Compressibility: Gases can be easily compressed, meaning their volume can be significantly reduced by applying pressure.

Expansibility: Gases have no definite shape or volume and expand to fill any container they are placed in.

Diffusion: Gases readily mix with other gases due to the rapid and random motion of their particles.

Low Density: Gases have very low densities compared to liquids and solids due to the large spaces between their particles.
